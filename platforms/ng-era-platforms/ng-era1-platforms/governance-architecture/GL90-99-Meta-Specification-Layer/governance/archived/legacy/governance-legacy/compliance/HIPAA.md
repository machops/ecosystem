## 1. Overview

This policy establishes the MachineNativeOps framework for compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996, including the Privacy Rule, Security Rule, and Breach Notification Rule. This policy applies to all personnel and systems that create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information (PHI).

### 1.1 Scope

This policy applies to:
- All MachineNativeOps employees, contractors, and business associates
- All electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI) processed, stored, or transmitted
- All systems, applications, and networks handling PHI
- All business associate agreements with third-party vendors

### 1.2 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:
- Ensure compliance with HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI
- Establish safeguards for PHI handling
- Define breach notification procedures
- Minimize risks to PHI

## 2. Privacy Rule Compliance

### 2.1 Protected Health Information (PHI)

PHI includes any individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium, including:
- Demographic information
- Medical history
- Test results
- Insurance information
- Any information that identifies an individual and relates to their health condition

### 2.2 Permitted Uses and Disclosures

PHI may be used or disclosed without authorization for:
- **Treatment**: Providing healthcare to the individual
- **Payment**: Obtaining payment for healthcare services
- **Healthcare Operations**: Administrative, financial, legal, and quality improvement activities
- **Public Health**: Required by law for public health authorities
- **Research**: Under specific conditions with privacy protections
- **Law Enforcement**: As required by law
- **Vital Interests**: To prevent serious harm to individuals

### 2.3 Required Authorizations

Written authorization is required for any use or disclosure of PHI not otherwise permitted, including:
- Psychotherapy notes
- Marketing communications
- Sale of PHI
- Employment purposes

### 2.4 Minimum Necessary Standard

MachineNativeOps implements the minimum necessary standard:
- Only the minimum amount of PHI necessary to accomplish the intended purpose is used, disclosed, or requested
- Access to PHI is restricted based on job role and responsibilities
- Regular reviews are conducted to ensure compliance

## 3. Security Rule Compliance

### 3.1 Administrative Safeguards

#### 3.1.1 Security Management Process
- **Risk Analysis**: Regular and periodic analysis of risks to ePHI
- **Risk Management**: Implement security measures to reduce risks to reasonable and appropriate levels
- **Sanction Policy**: Policies and procedures regarding sanctions for non-compliance
- **Information System Activity Review**: Regular review of system activity records

#### 3.1.2 Assigned Security Responsibility
- Designate a Security Officer responsible for developing and implementing policies
- Ensure security responsibilities are assigned to appropriate personnel

#### 3.1.3 Workforce Security
- **Authorization and Supervision**: Policies for authorizing access to ePHI
- **Workforce Clearance Procedure**: Procedures for determining clearance levels
- **Termination Procedures**: Access termination upon employment or contract end

#### 3.1.4 Information Access Management
- Implement policies for access authorization and establishment
- Implement procedures for access review and modification

#### 3.1.5 Security Awareness and Training
- Implement security awareness and training programs for all workforce members
- Conduct periodic security updates

#### 3.1.6 Security Incident Procedures
- Implement procedures to detect and report security incidents
- Implement response and mitigation procedures

#### 3.1.7 Contingency Plan
- **Data Backup Plan**: Procedures for creating and maintaining backups of ePHI
- **Disaster Recovery Plan**: Procedures for restoring lost data
- **Emergency Mode Operation Plan**: Procedures for enabling continuation of critical business functions
- **Testing and Revision**: Periodic testing and revision of contingency plans

#### 3.1.8 Evaluation
- Perform periodic technical and non-technical evaluations of security safeguards

#### 3.1.9 Business Associate Contracts and Other Arrangements
- Ensure business associates comply with HIPAA requirements through written contracts or arrangements

### 3.2 Physical Safeguards

#### 3.2.1 Facility Access Controls
- **Contingency Operations**: Establish procedures for access in emergencies
- **Facility Security Plan**: Implement policies and procedures to protect physical facilities
- **Access Control and Validation**: Procedures to control and validate access
- **Maintenance Records**: Document repairs and modifications to facilities

#### 3.2.2 Workstation Use
- Implement policies specifying proper use of workstations with ePHI access

#### 3.2.3 Workstation Security
- Implement physical safeguards for workstations that access ePHI

#### 3.2.4 Device and Media Controls
- **Disposal and Re-use**: Procedures for disposal and re-use of electronic media
- **Media Movement**: Procedures for movement of electronic media
- **Data Backup and Storage**: Secure storage and backup of electronic media

### 3.3 Technical Safeguards

#### 3.3.1 Access Control
- **Unique User Identification**: Assign unique user names/numbers for tracking identity
- **Emergency Access Procedure**: Obtain necessary ePHI during emergencies
- **Automatic Logoff**: Automatic termination of electronic sessions after idle time
- **Encryption and Decryption**: Implement encryption mechanisms for ePHI

#### 3.3.2 Audit Controls
- Implement hardware, software, and procedural mechanisms to record and examine activity in information systems containing ePHI

#### 3.3.3 Integrity Controls
- Implement policies and procedures to protect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction

#### 3.3.4 Transmission Security
- Implement technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to ePHI transmitted over electronic networks

## 4. Business Associate Agreements

### 4.1 Required Agreements

MachineNativeOps requires written business associate agreements with:
- Any third-party vendor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of MachineNativeOps
- Cloud service providers storing or processing ePHI
- Subcontractors of business associates

### 4.2 Agreement Content

Business associate agreements must include:
- Permitted and required uses of PHI
- Prohibition on further use or disclosure of PHI beyond permitted uses
- Implementation of appropriate safeguards
- Reporting of breaches and security incidents
- Access to PHI for compliance review
- Termination procedures

### 4.3 Compliance Monitoring

MachineNativeOps monitors business associate compliance through:
- Annual compliance reviews
- Security assessments
- Regular communications
- Right to audit provisions

## 5. Breach Notification

### 5.1 Breach Definition

A breach is the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of unsecured PHI in a manner not permitted under HIPAA that compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.

### 5.2 Breach Notification Requirements

#### 5.2.1 Notification to Individuals
- Without unreasonable delay, and no later than 60 calendar days after discovery
- If unsecured PHI involves 500 or more individuals, notify prominent media outlets
- If fewer than 500 individuals, notify within 60 days of the end of the calendar year

#### 5.2.2 Notification to HHS
- For breaches affecting 500 or more individuals: notify without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days
- For breaches affecting fewer than 500 individuals: notify annually

### 5.3 Breach Assessment

When assessing whether a breach has occurred, consider:
- The nature and extent of the PHI involved
- The unauthorized person who used the PHI or to whom it was disclosed
- Whether the PHI was actually acquired or viewed
- The extent to which the risk to PHI has been mitigated
