# Code of Conduct

- **Version:** 1.0.0

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of behavior and ethical expectations for all individuals participating in MachineNativeOps communities, including employees, contractors, contributors, users, and community members. Our goal is to foster an inclusive, respectful, and collaborative environment where everyone can thrive.

### 1.2 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All MachineNativeOps employees, contractors, and consultants
- All community members and contributors to open source projects
- All users of MachineNativeOps products and services
- All company-sponsored events, meetings, and communications
- All online platforms, including forums, chat channels, and social media

### 1.3 Commitment

MachineNativeOps is committed to providing a welcoming and safe environment for everyone, regardless of:
- Age
- Body size
- Caste
- Disability
- Ethnicity
- Family status
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Nationality
- Physical appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual orientation
- Socioeconomic status
- Veteran status
- Any other dimension of diversity

---

## 2. Our Standards

### 2.1 Expected Behavior

We expect all participants to contribute to a positive and productive environment by:

- **Being Respectful**: Treat others with dignity and respect, valuing diverse perspectives and experiences
- **Being Inclusive**: Welcome and encourage participation from all backgrounds and skill levels
- **Being Collaborative**: Work together constructively, sharing knowledge and supporting others
- **Being Professional**: Maintain professional conduct in all interactions and communications
- **Being Constructive**: Provide feedback in a helpful and respectful manner
- **Being Empathetic**: Consider the impact of words and actions on others
- **Being Accountable**: Take responsibility for your actions and their consequences

### 2.2 Positive Contributions

We encourage and appreciate contributions that:
- Help others learn and grow
- Share knowledge and expertise
- Improve our products and documentation
- Report issues and bugs responsibly
- Participate in discussions thoughtfully
- Mentor and support newcomers
- Celebrate others' successes

### 2.3 Open Communication

We value open, honest, and transparent communication:
- Share ideas and concerns constructively
- Listen actively to others' viewpoints
- Ask questions to understand different perspectives
- Provide context and reasoning for decisions
- Acknowledge and learn from mistakes

---

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

### 3.1 Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment
- **Discrimination**: Unfair treatment based on protected characteristics
- **Retaliation**: Adverse action against individuals who report violations or participate in investigations
- **Violence or Threats**: Physical violence or threats of violence
- **Sexual Harassment**: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal/physical conduct of a sexual nature
- **Bullying**: Repeated, unreasonable behavior that creates a risk to health and safety
- **Stalking**: Repeated, unwanted attention or contact
- **Doxxing**: Publishing private information without consent
- **Hate Speech**: Language that promotes violence, discrimination, or hostility against protected groups

### 3.2 Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

Specific examples include, but are not limited to:
- Offensive comments, jokes, or name-calling
- Sexual images or content in public spaces
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Unwelcome physical contact
- Persistent unwanted attention or advances
- Derogatory language or slurs
- Displaying sexually explicit or violent material
- Inappropriate use of nudity or sexual images
- Encouraging others to engage in prohibited behavior

### 3.3 Online Conduct

Prohibited behaviors apply equally to online interactions:
- Inappropriate content in chat rooms, forums, or social media
- Harassment via direct messages or comments
- Trolling or disruptive behavior
- Spamming or flooding channels
- Impersonating others
- Sharing private communications without consent

## 4. Reporting and Enforcement

### 4.1 Reporting Process

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Immediate Safety**: If anyone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services
2. **Document the Incident**: Record details including date, time, location, and individuals involved
3. **Report to Appropriate Channel**:
   - For work-related issues: Contact HR or your manager
   - For community issues: Email conduct@machinenativeops.io
   - For anonymous reporting: Use our anonymous reporting form
   - For urgent matters: Call our conduct hotline at 1-800-XXX-XXXX

### 4.2 What to Include in Your Report

When reporting, please provide:
- Your contact information (optional for anonymous reports)
- The name(s) of the individual(s) involved
- Description of the behavior or incident
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Any witnesses or evidence
- Any actions you have already taken

### 4.3 Response Timeline

We commit to:
- Acknowledging receipt of reports within 24 hours
- Beginning investigations within 48 hours
- Providing updates on investigation progress
- Resolving most cases within 7 business days
- Communicating outcomes to relevant parties (respecting confidentiality)

### 4.4 Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with strict confidentiality:
- Information shared only on a need-to-know basis
- Protection of reporter identity to the extent possible
- Secure storage of all documentation
- Compliance with applicable privacy laws

### 4.5 Investigation Process

Our investigation process includes:
1. **Initial Review**: Assess the report and determine appropriate response
2. **Fact-Finding**: Gather information from all parties involved
3. **Analysis**: Evaluate the information against this Code of Conduct
4. **Determination**: Determine if a violation occurred and appropriate action
5. **Action**: Implement corrective measures
6. **Follow-Up**: Monitor the situation and ensure resolution

---

## 5. Consequences

### 5.1 Enforcement Actions

Violations of this Code of Conduct will result in appropriate consequences, which may include:

- **Verbal Warning**: Informal discussion about the behavior and expectations
- **Written Warning**: Formal documented warning outlining the issue
- **Removal from Specific Activities**: Exclusion from certain events, meetings, or channels
- **Temporary Suspension**: Limited access to facilities or systems for a specified period
- **Termination**: End of employment, contract, or participation in community
- **Legal Action**: Referral to law enforcement when appropriate
- **Public Statement**: Announcement of actions taken (when necessary for community safety)

### 5.2 Factors Considered

When determining consequences, we consider:
- Severity and impact of the behavior
- Intent and pattern of behavior
- Previous history of violations
- Cooperation with investigation
- Impact on victims and community
- Consistency with previous enforcement actions

### 5.3 Appeals Process

Individuals may appeal enforcement decisions by:
- Submitting a written appeal to conduct@machinenativeops.io within 5 business days
- Clearly stating the reason for the appeal
- Providing additional evidence or context
- Appeals will be reviewed by a different investigator or committee

## 6. Community Guidelines

### 6.1 Collaboration

We encourage collaboration that is:
- Open and transparent
- Respectful of different perspectives
- Focused on shared goals
- Constructive and solution-oriented
- Inclusive of diverse viewpoints

### 6.2 Conflict Resolution

When conflicts arise:
- Address issues directly and respectfully with the person involved
- Focus on the problem, not the person
- Seek to understand before seeking to be understood
- Escalate to moderators or leadership if unable to resolve
- Document conflicts for future reference

### 6.3 Event Participation

At events and meetings:
- Respect everyone's time and attention
- Be prepared and contribute meaningfully
- Allow others to speak and share
- Follow event-specific guidelines
- Report any concerns to event organizers

---

## 7. Professional Responsibilities

### 7.1 Ethical Conduct

All participants are expected to:
- Act with integrity and honesty
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Protect confidential information
- Comply with applicable laws and regulations
- Report ethical concerns appropriately

### 7.2 Intellectual Property

Respect intellectual property rights:
- Attribute sources appropriately
- Obtain permissions before using others' work
- Respect licenses and copyrights
- Contribute original work or clearly mark derivatives

### 7.3 Data Privacy

Protect personal and sensitive information:
- Follow data handling procedures
- Obtain consent before sharing personal data
- Respect privacy settings and preferences
- Report data breaches immediately

---

## 8. Leadership Responsibilities

### 8.1 Role Modeling

Leaders and managers are expected to:
- Demonstrate exemplary behavior
- Promote this Code of Conduct actively
- Address violations promptly and fairly
- Create safe and inclusive environments
- Support those who report violations

### 8.2 Training and Education

MachineNativeOps will provide:
- Code of Conduct training for all employees
- Regular refreshers and updates
- Resources for conflict resolution
- Training on unconscious bias and inclusivity

### 8.3 Continuous Improvement

We commit to:
- Regular review of this Code of Conduct
- Updating based on community feedback
- Learning from incidents and evolving best practices
- Measuring effectiveness of our policies
