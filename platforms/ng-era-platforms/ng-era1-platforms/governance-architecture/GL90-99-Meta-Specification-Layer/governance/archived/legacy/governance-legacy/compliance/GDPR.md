## 1. Overview

This policy outlines the MachineNativeOps approach to compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU 2016/679) for processing personal data of EU citizens. This policy applies to all personnel, contractors, and third parties processing personal data on behalf of MachineNativeOps.

### 1.1 Scope

This policy applies to:
- All MachineNativeOps employees, contractors, and agents
- All processing activities involving personal data of EU data subjects
- All systems and platforms managed by MachineNativeOps that process personal data

### 1.2 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:
- Ensure compliance with GDPR requirements
- Protect the privacy rights of data subjects
- Establish clear procedures for data processing
- Mitigate risks associated with data breaches
- Demonstrate accountability through documentation

---

## 2. Data Protection Principles

MachineNativeOps is committed to the following data protection principles:

### 2.1 Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency

Personal data shall be processed lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner. All processing activities must have a lawful basis under Article 6 of GDPR, including:
- Consent of the data subject
- Performance of a contract
- Legal obligation
- Vital interests
- Public task
- Legitimate interests

### 2.2 Purpose Limitation

Personal data shall be collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes.

### 2.3 Data Minimization

Personal data shall be adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which they are processed.

### 2.4 Accuracy

Personal data shall be accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date. Every reasonable step must be taken to ensure that inaccurate data are erased or rectified without delay.

### 2.5 Storage Limitation

Personal data shall be kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data are processed.

### 2.6 Integrity and Confidentiality

Personal data shall be processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage.

### 2.7 Accountability

The data controller shall be responsible for, and be able to demonstrate, compliance with the data protection principles.

## 3. Data Subject Rights

MachineNativeOps respects and facilitates the following data subject rights:

### 3.1 Right to be Informed

Data subjects have the right to be informed about the collection and use of their personal data. This is achieved through:
- Clear privacy notices
- Transparent communication about data processing
- Information provided at the time of collection

### 3.2 Right of Access

Data subjects have the right to access their personal data and supplementary information. Access requests must be responded to within one month.

### 3.3 Right to Rectification

Data subjects have the right to have inaccurate personal data rectified and, taking into account the purposes of the processing, to have incomplete personal data completed.

### 3.4 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

Data subjects have the right to request the deletion of their personal data when:
- The data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected
- Consent is withdrawn
- The data subject objects to processing
- The data was processed unlawfully

### 3.5 Right to Restrict Processing

Data subjects have the right to restrict processing when:
- Accuracy is contested
- Processing is unlawful but the subject opposes erasure
- The controller no longer needs the data but the subject requires it for legal claims
- An objection is pending

### 3.6 Right to Data Portability

Data subjects have the right to receive their personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.

### 3.7 Right to Object

Data subjects have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

### 3.8 Rights in Relation to Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

Data subjects have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal or similarly significant effects.

## 4. Data Breach Response

### 4.1 Breach Identification

A personal data breach is a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data.

### 4.2 Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach:
- Notify the supervisory authority without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than 72 hours after becoming aware of the breach
- Communicate the breach to the data subject without undue delay when the breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals

### 4.3 Breach Response Procedure

1. **Immediate Assessment**
   - Identify the nature and scope of the breach
   - Determine the types of data affected
   - Assess the potential impact on data subjects

2. **Containment**
   - Take immediate steps to contain the breach
   - Prevent further unauthorized access
   - Preserve evidence for investigation

3. **Notification**
   - Prepare breach notification to supervisory authority
   - Determine if data subject notification is required
   - Notify affected parties according to GDPR timelines

4. **Investigation**
   - Conduct thorough investigation of the breach
   - Identify root cause
   - Document findings and remediation steps

5. **Remediation**
   - Implement corrective actions
   - Update policies and procedures as needed
   - Provide training to prevent recurrence

## 5. Data Protection by Design and Default

### 5.1 Data Protection by Design

MachineNativeOps implements data protection measures into the design of systems and processes from the outset. This includes:
- Conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing
- Implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures
- Regularly reviewing and updating data protection practices

### 5.2 Data Protection by Default

By default, MachineNativeOps systems and processes are configured to:
- Only process personal data necessary for each specific purpose
- Automatically limit access to personal data to authorized personnel
- Provide data subjects with control over their personal data

---

## 6. International Data Transfers

### 6.1 Adequacy Decisions

Personal data may be transferred to countries outside the EEA that have been deemed adequate by the European Commission.

### 6.2 Appropriate Safeguards

When transferring data to countries without adequacy decisions, MachineNativeOps ensures appropriate safeguards are in place, including:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
- Approved codes of conduct or certification mechanisms

### 6.3 Derogations

Transfers may be made in specific circumstances with appropriate protections, including:
- Explicit consent from the data subject
- Performance of a contract
- Important public interest
- Legal claims

## 7. Compliance Monitoring

### 7.1 Regular Audits

MachineNativeOps conducts regular audits to ensure GDPR compliance:
- Annual compliance review
- Data processing activity audits
- Third-party vendor assessments
- Security and privacy controls testing

### 7.2 Records of Processing Activities

MachineNativeOps maintains comprehensive records of all processing activities under its responsibility, including:
- Purposes of processing
- Categories of data subjects and personal data
- Recipients of personal data
- Transfers to third countries
- Retention schedules

### 7.3 Training

All personnel handling personal data receive GDPR training:
- Mandatory annual training for all staff
- Role-specific training for data processors
- Refresher training on policy updates
